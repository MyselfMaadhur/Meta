1. Division of Ancient History and Sources

Core Idea: History is all about understanding the past. Sources are crucial to unravel and understand the past.

1. Three Divisions of History

History

Written evidences available
From Vedic Phase till present

Prehistory

No written evidence
Only Archaeological evidences available
Example: Stone age

Proto-history

Written evidences available
But script could not be deciphered
Example: Harappan Period

2. Sources of History

Tap each card to see details:

Archaeological Evidence
Artefacts, tools, pottery, burials, settlements, architectural remains. Study of materials left by ancient people.
Inscriptions
Engravings on stone or metal. Rock edicts, pillar edicts. Study = Epigraphy
Coins
Study = Numismatics. Show rulers, economy, trade. First gold coins by Indo-Greek king Menander
Literature
Indian + Foreign texts. Vedas, Puranas, Epics, Sangam works, plays, travel accounts
Foreign Accounts
Writings by foreign travelers. Give info on admin, society, economy, trade relations
Indian Literature - Full List
  • Vedic Literature: Vedas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads, Vedangas, Upvedas
  • Puranas: Stories of kings + genealogies of dynasties. Key source for political history
  • Epics: Ramayana, Mahabharata
  • Sangam Literature: Tolkappiyam, Ettuogai, Pattupattu, Pathinenkilkanakku, Silpadikarm, Manimekkalai
  • Plays: Works by Kalidas + other writings of Navaratnas in Gupta court

Archaeological Evidence - Details
  • Artefacts: Materials used by ancient people found at sites. Used to understand prehistoric period
  • Tools: Stone, bone tools + bronze and iron tools
  • Pottery: Black and Red Ware, Northern Black Polished Ware, Polished Grey Ware
  • Inscriptions: Engravings on stone/metal. Major Rock edicts, Minor Rock edicts, Pillar edicts
  • Other: Pollen analysis, burials with tools, settlements, architectural remains
