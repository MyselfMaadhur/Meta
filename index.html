Three Divisions of History

History vs Prehistory vs Proto-history

▼
📝
History
Written evidence available from Vedic phase till present
🪨
Prehistory
No written evidence; only archaeological (Stone Age)
❓
Proto-history
Written evidence exists but not deciphered (e.g., Harappan)

Sources of Ancient Indian History

5 Major categories of evidence

▼

🔬 Archaeological Evidence (Artefacts)

  • Physical materials used by ancient people found at sites
  • Crucial for understanding the Pre-historic Period

📜 Inscriptions (Epigraphy)

  • Engravings on stone or metal objects
  • Study of inscriptions = Epigraphy
  • Types: Rock edicts (Major & Minor), Pillar edicts

🪙 Coins (Numismatics)

  • Study of coins = Numismatics
  • Reveals economic conditions, trade, and rulers

📚 Literature

  • Indian Literature: Vedas, Puranas, Epics, Sangam texts
  • Foreign Literature: Accounts by Greek, Chinese travelers

🌍 Accounts of Foreigners

  • Greek, Chinese, and other foreign travelers' records
  • Provide external perspective on Indian conditions

1. Introduction & Naming

Discovery, names, and basic facts

▼

🔍 Discovery

  • First site excavated — Harappa by Dayaram Sahni in 1921
  • Name of the culture goes by the name of the first site excavated

📛 Alternative Names

  • Also called Indus Valley Civilization because most sites are found in the valley of River Indus
  • Also called Harappan Civilization

⏳ Age Comparison

  • Harappan Civilization is older to chalcolithic cultures but advanced when compared to them
🗺️

2. Geographical Area & Time Period

Extent, dates, and coverage

▼
📅
Time Period
Flourished between 2500–1500 BC
⭐
Mature Period
2200–1800 BC was its mature period
🌏
Coverage
Present Pakistan & North-western and western part of India

🗺️ Extent

  • Extended from Jammu in the North to the Narmada estuary in the South
  • Covers western part of UP and extends to Baluchistan
  • Forms the western boundary
🏙️

3. Town Planning & Structures

City layout, buildings, and remarkable features

▼

🏗️ City Division

  • City divided into two parts:
  • Upper Citadel — city on raised platform
  • Lower Citadel — lower town

📐 Grid System

  • Grid system followed in arrangement of houses and roads

🧱 Burnt Bricks

  • Usage of burnt bricks — a remarkable feature of Harappan

🏊 Great Bath

  • Great Bath served as ritual bathing
  • Found in upper citadel of Mohenjo-Daro

🌾 Granaries

  • A large granary in Mohenjo-Daro
  • Six granaries in Harappa

🏠 Barracks

  • Two-roomed barracks in Harappa
  • Possibly accommodated labourers

🚰 Drainage System

  • Drainage system was another remarkable feature of the civilization
🌾

4. Agriculture & Trade

Crops, irrigation, commerce, and weights

▼

🌱 Fertility

  • The Indus region was fertile in ancient times
  • Otherwise it would not have supported towns and villages

🌾 Crops Grown

  • Wheat
  • Barley
  • Peas
  • Sesame
  • Mustard
  • In Lothal, people started producing rice

👕 Cotton

  • Indus people were the first to produce cotton

💧 Irrigation

  • Structures used to store water — Gabarbands or Nalas
  • But channel or canal irrigation seems to have been absent

🚢 Trade

  • Indus valley people had both short and long-distance trade
  • Also foreign trade
  • Used boats and bullock-carts for transportation

💰 Currency & Exchange

  • No use of metallic currency
  • Practised barter system of exchange

⚖️ Weights & Measures

  • Developed uniform system of weights and measures

🔖 Seals & Sealings

  • Seals and sealings were used in trade
  • As marks of ownership meant to guarantee the quality of the product

▼

🌾 Mixed Farming

  • Oxen

  • Sheep

7. Religion & Religious Practices

Deities, worship, burials, and beliefs

▼

🏛️ Collective Worship

  • Presence of some structures with ritual significance indicate existence of collective worship

🏊 Great Bath

  • Great Bath, Mohenjo-Daro had a great ritual significance

🌱 Fertility Cult & Mother Goddess

  • Prevalence of cults of fertility and mother goddess worship

🌳 Tree Spirits

  • They worshipped tree spirits
  • Pipal tree was worshipped

🕉️ Proto-Shiva (Pashupati)

  • On the seals, a famous deity identified as proto-Shiva was found
  • The deity is surrounded by animals like goats, elephant, tiger, and antelope

🔱 Phallic Worship

  • There is sufficient evidence for the prevalence of phallic worship
  • Numerous stone symbols of female sex organs (yoni worship)
  • Besides those of phallus, have been discovered

🔥 Fire Worship

  • The worship of fire is proved by the discovery of fire altars
  • At Lothal, Kalibangan, and Harappa

👻 Beliefs

  • They believed in ghosts and evil forces
  • Used amulets as protection against them

⚰️ Disposal of the Dead

  • Disposal of the dead has been an important religious activity
  • Dead bodies were generally placed in the north-south direction
  • Nearby the dead body the ornaments and other objects were also buried
Key Feature

Fertility cult was the main feature of Harappan religion

5. Society: Early vs Later Vedic Period

Social structure, women, and varna system

▼

🏛️ Early Vedic Society

  • Kinship was the basis of social structure
  • People gave primary loyalty to the tribe (Jana)
  • Another important term for tribe: Vis
  • Term for family (kula) mentioned rarely
  • Family in early Vedic phase indicated by term griha

👩 Women in Early Vedic Period

  • Institution of marriage was established
  • Practice of levirate and widow remarriage in the Rig Veda
  • Status of women was equal to men
  • They received Upanayana and education
  • Studied Vedas; some rose to rank of seers composing Vedic hymns
  • Monogamy established, though polygamy and polyandry were also known

🎨 Varna System — Early Vedic

  • Varna was the term used for color
  • Aryans were fair; indigenous inhabitants were dark
  • The dasas and Dasyus, conquered by Aryans, were treated as slaves and sudras
  • Tribal society divided into 3 groups: warriors (Kshatriya), priests (Brahmins), Vaishyas
  • The fourth division called Shudras appeared towards the end of the Rig Veda period
  • Divisions based on occupations had started but not very sharp

🏛️ Later Vedic Society

  • Society came to be divided into 4 varnas: Brahmanas, Rajanyas/Kshatriyas, Vaisyas, Shudras
  • Each Varna was assigned with its duty
  • All 3 higher varnas shared one common feature: known as Dvijas (twice born)
  • Entitled to upanayana or investiture with the sacred thread
  • The fourth Varna was deprived of the sacred thread ceremony
  • With it began the imposition of disabilities on the shudras

🔗 Gotra System — Later Vedic

  • Institution of gotra appeared in later Vedic times
  • Literally means the cow-pen or place where cattle belonging to the whole clan are kept
  • People began to practice gotra exogamy
  • No marriage could take place between persons belonging to the same gotra or having the same ancestor

🧘 Ashrama System — Later Vedic

  • Ashramas or four stages of life were not well established in early Vedic times
  • In post-Vedic texts, four ashramas:
  • Brahmachari — student
  • Grihastha — householder
  • Vanaprastha — partial retirement
  • Sanyasa — complete retirement from the world
  • Only three mentioned in later Vedic texts
  • The 4th Ashrama only mentioned in Jabala Upanishad; not well-established in Later Vedic times

▼

  • All four Vedas/Samhitas
  • Commentaries on 4 Vedas — Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads
  • 6 Vedangas and 4 Upvedas
The Four Vedas
VedaKey FactsReciter
Rig Veda First text in world literature; theme is prayer addressed to gods; Gayatri mantra part of it; 1017 hymns into 10 mandalas Hotri

📜 Brahmanas

  • Related to conduct of various ceremonies
  • Prose version of the Vedas
  • 108 in number
  • Satapatha Brahmana — most famous; deals with rituals connected with agricultural production
  • Gopada Brahmana — most voluminous
  • Aithreya Brahmana — about Viswamitra and non-Aryan

🌲 Aranyakas

  • "Forest texts" written mainly for hermits and students living in forests
  • Concluding portions of Brahmanas
  • Considered as the link between Brahmana and Upanishad

🧠 Upanishads

  • 108 in number; also known as Vedantas
  • Deal with metaphysics

📐 Vedangas (6 Limbs)

  • Nirukta — Etymology; study of origin of words
  • Shiksha — Phonetics; oldest; deals with pronunciation
  • Chehendus (Chandas) — Metre; related to recitation
  • Vyakarana — Grammar; written by Panini
  • Kalpa — Rituals; basis of Indian law
  • Dharmashastras — deals with household ceremonies
  • Sulvasutras — deals with rekhaganita (Geometry)
  • Srutasutras — deals with social ceremonies (marriage, birth)
  • Jyotisha — Astronomy; study of movement of planets and understanding events in life

📖 Upvedas (4 Applied Sciences)

  • Dhanurveda (Warfare) — associated with Rig Veda
  • Gandharva Veda (Music) — associated with Sama Veda
  • Sthapatyaveda (Architecture) — associated with Yajur Veda
  • Ayurveda (Medicine) — associated with Atharva Veda
