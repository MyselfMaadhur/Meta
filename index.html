Three Divisions of History

History vs Prehistory vs Proto-history

▼
📝
History
Written evidence available from Vedic phase till present
🪨
Prehistory
No written evidence; only archaeological (Stone Age)
❓
Proto-history
Written evidence exists but not deciphered (e.g., Harappan)
🔍

Sources of Ancient Indian History

5 Major categories of evidence

▼

🔬 Archaeological Evidence (Artefacts)

  • Physical materials used by ancient people found at sites
  • Crucial for understanding the Pre-historic Period

📜 Inscriptions (Epigraphy)

  • Engravings on stone or metal objects
  • Study of inscriptions = Epigraphy
  • Types: Rock edicts (Major & Minor), Pillar edicts

🪙 Coins (Numismatics)

  • Study of coins = Numismatics
  • Reveals economic conditions, trade, and rulers

📚 Literature

  • Indian Literature: Vedas, Puranas, Epics, Sangam texts
  • Foreign Literature: Accounts by Greek, Chinese travelers

🌍 Accounts of Foreigners

  • Greek, Chinese, and other foreign travelers' records
  • Provide external perspective on Indian conditions

Indian Literature Sources

Vedic, Puranic, Epic & Sangam texts

▼
Must Remember

Vedic Literature: Vedas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads, Vedangas, Upvedas

CategoryKey DetailsImportance
Vedas Rig, Yajur, Sama, Atharva Oldest religious texts; Rigveda = first world literature
Brahmanas Prose commentaries on Vedas Explain rituals and ceremonies
Aranyakas "Forest texts" for hermits Bridge between Brahmanas & Upanishads
Upanishads 108 in number; Vedantas Deal with metaphysics & philosophy
Vedangas 6 limbs: Shiksha, Kalpa, Vyakarana, Nirukta, Chandas, Jyotisha Ancillary sciences to understand Vedas
Upvedas Dhanurveda, Gandharvaveda, Sthapatyaveda, Ayurveda Applied knowledge: warfare, music, architecture, medicine

📜 Puranas

  • Stories of kings & genealogies of dynasties
  • Important source for political history of Ancient India

⚔️ Epics

  • Ramayana & Mahabharata
  • Reflect social, political & moral values of the time

🌴 Sangam Literature

  • Tolkappiyam (Tamil grammar)
  • Ettutogai (8 anthologies)
  • Pattupattu (10 poems)
  • Pathinenkilkanakku (18 minor works)
  • Silappadikaram & Manimekkalai (two epics)

🎭 Plays & Drama

  • Kalidasa's works (Navaratna of Gupta court)
  • Visakha Datta's Mudra Rakshas (Gupta period, Chanakya-Chandragupta story)

Archaeological Evidences

Artefacts, tools, pottery & burials

▼

🔨 Tools

  • Stone & bone tools
  • Metal tools: Bronze & Iron

🏺 Pottery Types

  • Black & Red Ware
  • Northern Black Polished Ware (NBPW) — Important for Mahajanapada & Mauryan period
  • Polished Grey Ware (PGW)
  • Ochre Coloured Pottery (OCP)

🌸 Pollen Analysis

  • Analysis of floral remains to understand ancient climate & vegetation

⚰️ Burials

  • Burial sites with tools found nearby
  • Reveals belief systems & social status

🏛️ Settlements & Architecture

  • Structural remains of houses, forts, public buildings
  • Reveals town planning & social organization

Inscriptions

Types & key examples

▼

📝 Definition

  • Engravings on stone or metal objects
  • Study of inscriptions = Epigraphy

📍 Types of Edicts

  • Rock Edicts: Major and Minor — engraved on rocks
  • Pillar Edicts: Engraved on pillars (e.g., Ashokan pillars)
  • Cave Inscriptions: Found in caves (e.g., Barabar caves)
Important Example

Allahabad Pillar Inscription — Famous for Samudragupta's prashasti (eulogy) by Harisena
